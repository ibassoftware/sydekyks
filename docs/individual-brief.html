The choice

Two ways to join, not two tiers of one.

I want this line to be sharp, because a fuzzy version breeds resentment later. A Growth Partner owns a function: the commercial engine, sourcing and closing. A co-founder shares the whole thing: the entire risk and the entire vision, across every problem, for the long haul. Different jobs. Different rewards. Different levels of say.

Why these roles exist, honestly

The technology is already strong and shipping, and partners are already onboarding. The scarce thing here is not more engineering, it's commercial reach: the person who builds the sales channel. That is what a Growth Partner owns a piece of, and what a co-founder would own at company level. I'm not looking for someone to help me build the product; I'm looking for the person who takes sales and makes it theirs.

Track A

Growth Partner.
Skin in the game, rewarded fast for it.

For someone who turns their network into deals quickly, is energized by the bet, and would rather back their own upside than settle for salary security. You own the commercial engine: sourcing and closing. It's a commercial role, but not a mercenary one: the people who do well here close because they believe in what they're putting in front of a customer.

How you're rewarded, in the order I'll always be honest about

Commission, real and recurring recurring, climbs to 40%

The recurring commission ladder you just saw, on every deal you source and close, paid monthly for the life of the account. It should be real income you can count on, because I'd rather pay you properly than ask you to run on promise and passion alone.

Equity, upside you unlock proposed: ~0.5%, at $50k signed ARR

This opens once you've brought $50,000 in signed ARR: real, paying customers, not introductions. From there it's a defined stake via a signed forward promise that crystallizes into an actual BSA at spin-out or first raise, with vesting counted from your start date. Commission rewards every deal from day one; the equity is what you unlock by proving you can close, and, I hope, because you believe in where this is going.

The protections, both ways

Equity vests against milestones and performance where we can define them, with a continued-engagement condition and a lapse clause, so unearned upside doesn't stick to someone who drifts away. No board control or co-founder decision rights; the equity becomes actual shares only when exercised, at a liquidity event. Fair to you, fair to the company.

Sydekyks · An Invitation · Track A07
Track B

Co-founder: earned, not granted.

A real co-founder stake of 3% to 10%, earned, not granted. Everyone starts as a Growth Partner and proves themselves first; co-founder is a partnership both people choose, not a title handed out. In practice this seat leads the sales side of the company: the channel, the partner network, the revenue engine. The technology is already strong, so what's open is commercial ownership. A second co-founder will likely lead marketing, so you would own sales and revenue, not all of go-to-market. Here's the exact mechanic.

The two-gate model
Gate 1
1

Eligibility Objective · yours

A concrete, time-bound performance bar. My proposal: you source and close $200,000 in signed recurring contracts within 6 to 9 months. Clear it and you've proven the commercial thesis. Necessary, but not sufficient on its own.

Gate 2
2

Invitation Fit & trust

Clearing Gate 1 opens the conversation. Whether it becomes an invitation comes down to fit and trust: would we both operate like owners, and trust each other through the hard months. I carry the final say, the same way you should want final say over whose company you tie your name to. It is not ranking you from above; it is both of us deciding we want this for years.

"Hitting the number opens the door. Walking through it is something we decide together."
And if it doesn't lead to co-founder

Then nothing you've built goes away. You stay a Growth Partner and keep everything you've earned, your commission and your Track A equity, in full. The two phases are separate by design, so a "not now" on co-founder never costs you what you've already won.

Vesting on the stake

A fresh, standard co-founder schedule: 4 years, 1-year cliff, counted from the promotion date, not backdated. Your trial-period work is already paid by Track A commission and equity.

What the 3 to 10% means

A share of Sydekyks at promotion (pre-raise), diluting in future rounds, not a fixed figure forever. Real say comes here too: shareholders' agreement, decision rights, likely a board seat.

Sydekyks · An Invitation · Track B08
The tension I won't hide

Commission is the paycheck.
The equity is the dream.

Because that final step comes down to mutual fit and not just a number, co-founder status is, by design, not guaranteed, and it should be. This cuts both ways: by the time we're having that conversation, you should be judging just as hard whether you want to tie yourself to me and this company for years. That honesty puts the weight on the near term: what you earn on Track A has to stand on its own, whether or not the co-founder conversation ever happens. So I'm keeping both real. None of this is only about money; the money just has to be honest before anything else can be. The commission is generous and fast because it's your actual income and because I'd rather respect your time than romanticize it. The 3 to 10% is a genuine, defined destination, not a carrot on a stick. If either were fake, "prove it and we'll talk" would just be a bait-and-switch, and I'm not interested in starting a relationship that way.

The deal in one breath

Start on Track A. You're rewarded well and quickly for the deals you close, on a recurring commission that climbs to 40%, and you earn a stake as you prove yourself. If you perform and we fit as owners, there's a real path to a co-founder stake and a seat at the table. If not, you keep everything you earned and we both walk away clean, no hard feelings.

How we paper it, cleanly and early
  • Everything in writing while we're both optimistic: roles, decision rights, equity, vesting, and leaver terms, agreed up front, not left to a handshake.
  • Near-term structure: the work runs through iBAS on a commission contract, with a one-page equity promise attached to the future Sydekyks entity, not to iBAS.
  • Formalities happen once, at spin-out or first raise, when the actual shares and instruments are issued.
  • Sydekyks IP, brand, and platform stay cleanly owned and transferable out of iBAS from the start, so the equity you're promised lives in the thing that actually grows.
